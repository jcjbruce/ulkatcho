### Approach 1: "Obsidian & Ember" — Dark Land Sovereignty

**Design Movement:** Contemporary Indigenous Brutalism meets Pacific Northwest Wilderness

**Core Principles:**
- Deep, dark, rooted — the site feels like standing in old-growth forest at dusk
- Asymmetric editorial layouts — content bleeds edge-to-edge, no sterile centering
- Obsidian as the dominant surface: near-black backgrounds with warm earth highlights
- Typography as land — wide, heavy, carved-looking display text

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: deep obsidian `#0D1208` (near-black with green undertone)
- Forest green: `#2D4A1E` — mid-ground, section dividers
- Earth brown: `#8B5E3C` — warm accent, card borders
- Birch white: `#F5EDD6` — primary text on dark
- Indian paintbrush red: `#C0392B` — accent CTA, alert banners
- Pine needle: `#4A6741` — secondary elements

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Full-bleed hero with parallax landscape photography
- Asymmetric two-column sections: wide image left, narrow text right (or reversed)
- Horizontal scrolling "land strip" for cultural elements
- Cards with left-edge color bars (earth brown or paintbrush red)

**Signature Elements:**
- Thin horizontal rule lines in earth brown between sections
- Subtle grain/noise texture overlay on dark backgrounds
- Geometric diamond/arrowhead motifs as section dividers

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Slow, deliberate — like moving through forest
- Hover states reveal content gradually (opacity transitions)
- Scroll-triggered fade-ins from below

**Animation:**
- Hero: slow parallax scroll (0.3x speed) on landscape image
- Section entries: fade-up with 0.6s ease-out, 0.1s stagger
- Nav: smooth underline slide on hover
- Cards: subtle lift (translateY -4px) with shadow deepening

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display (serif, bold) — dignified, timeless
- Body: Source Serif 4 (readable serif) — warm, editorial
- Labels/caps: Raleway (geometric sans) — clean contrast

### Approach 2: "River Stone & Birch" — Warm Earth Editorial ✅ CHOSEN

**Design Movement:** Pacific Northwest Land Journal — editorial warmth meets territorial pride

**Core Principles:**
- Warm, grounded, welcoming — like a community gathering hall
- Rich earth tones dominate; the land is always present
- Strong editorial hierarchy: large serif headings, generous whitespace
- Asymmetric but structured — not chaotic, but not corporate grid either

**Color Philosophy:**
- Background: warm birch white `#F7F2E8` — like aged paper, not sterile white
- Deep forest: `#1C3A1A` — primary dark, nav, headings
- Earth brown: `#7A4F2E` — warm secondary, borders
- Indian paintbrush: `#B8341B` — accent, CTAs, emergency banner
- Stone grey: `#8A8478` — muted text, captions
- Obsidian: `#1A1A18` — footer, deep contrast sections
- Sage: `#6B8C5A` — subtle highlights

**Layout Paradigm:**
- Full-bleed hero with slow parallax
- Alternating asymmetric sections: text-heavy left with image right, then reversed
- "Land strip" — full-width image band between sections
- Document cards in a clean grid with left accent bars
- Footer: dark obsidian with warm text

**Signature Elements:**
- Thin horizontal rules in earth brown
- Subtle warm paper texture on light sections
- Arrowhead/diamond decorative elements in paintbrush red

**Interaction Philosophy:**
- Warm and unhurried — like turning pages of a community journal
- Hover: gentle color shifts, no jarring transforms
- Active nav items: underline in paintbrush red

**Animation:**
- Hero: slow parallax (0.4x) on background image
- Sections: fade-up on scroll (0.5s ease-out)
- Cards: subtle shadow lift on hover
- Emergency banner: gentle pulse on paintbrush red

**Typography System:**
- Display: Playfair Display Bold — strong, dignified serif
- Body: Lora (serif) — warm, highly readable

## Selected Approach: **"River Stone & Birch"**

Warm birch paper backgrounds, deep forest green headings, earth brown accents, Indian paintbrush red CTAs. Playfair Display for headings, Lora for body. Asymmetric editorial layout. The site should feel like a beautifully printed community journal — dignified, warm, and deeply rooted in the land.
